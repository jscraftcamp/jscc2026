## 3 Models

### Ideal of Customer Profile – Problem – Cost of Delay

- It's difficult when there's no cost of delay: no pressure to solve the problem – so no need?
- A smaller target group (focus: e.g., only producers of XY in the Landshut area) to better understand their needs and, by actually solving their problems 👉 improve sales

🚨 Clarify the **need** to know what to sell



### Opener Setter Closing Prinzip

3 different groups (or even individuals, if necessary), ideally including one-on-one conversations

1. Opener: **Initial contact** (the goal is to establish contact, not to make a sale; rather, a brief call to discuss any issues)
   - Cold Calling
   - Conferences
   - company get-togethers ("Unternehmensstammtische")
   - ...

2. Setter: **Qualification**
   - “Do you have a problem I can help you solve?”
   - Don’t try to sell anything: Am I the right person to talk to? If necessary, refer them to someone else.
3. Closer: **Presentation** of a developed **solution** 👉 Sale 🤞

By the numbers: ~**500** people at the opening => ~**50** during the set-up phase => ~**8** during the closing phase => **1** sale?!



## Good to know

- It is generally assumed that a small business needs about 8 points of contact before a sale is made
- A lot of trial and error in sales: a friendly interaction or a referral can sometimes pay off in the long run (ramp-up phase)
- as a freelancer: you don't have to deal alone with those issues (everyone has the same struggles); if you're a technical person maybe team up with salesy one 🤝
- Meetup (with other Devs doesn't work as well), rather do: company get-togethers ("Unternehmensstammtische"), speaking at conferences (rather *passive* though)
- if the market is rough, cold calling and *active* (pre-)sales are necessary
- easier to sell 'snackable' pieces first:small initial – paid – workshop; start with POC and see if it takes off 👉 then do the heavy lifting (investment/offers 💸)
